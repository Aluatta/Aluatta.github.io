Visual Hierachy

The visual hierarchy of a 2D display (webpage, graphic, print, etc.) refers to the organization of the design elements on the page so that the eye is guided to consume each design element in the order of intended importance.

Alignment: Creates order within the design and guides the viewer’s eye around the page.
Based on Gutenberg’s diagram , the most important information should be placed in the upper left and the bottom right corner because viewer skims the page in a Z-pattern / F-pattern.

Gestalt

Psychological principles influence humans visual perception

We like to find simplicity and order in complex shapes because it demands the least cognitive effort from us. Designers can use these principles to prevent users from becoming overwhelmed with information.

  • Grouping rule: Proximity & Common Region
  • Similarity
  • Continuity
  • Closure
  • Figure-Ground / Negativ Space
  • Common Fate

Grouping princip: we perceive elements that are closer to each other to belong in the same group. In the same way, if elements are distant, human catalogs them into a different, separate groups. Without groupings, it would be a lot more difficult to understand where standard areas such as navigation, content or ads are thus, it would be harder to figure out where to direct attention. Psychology plays powerful role in visual perception.
The most important are: proximity, common region and similarity. Figure -ground is very ofen use in logos.
